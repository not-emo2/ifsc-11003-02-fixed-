University of Arkansas at Little Rock: A Brief History

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) is a public research university in Little Rock, Arkansas. Established as Little Rock Junior College by the Little Rock School District in 1927, the institution became a private four-year university under the name Little Rock University in 1957. It returned to public status in 1969 when it merged with the University of Arkansas System under its present name. The former campus of Little Rock Junior College is now (2019) the campus of Philander Smith University.

At 250 acres (100 ha), the UA Little Rock campus encompasses more than 56 buildings, including the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Sciences, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and the Ottenheimer Library Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities that include a learning resource center, art galleries, KUAR public radio station,[6] University Television, and a campus-wide wireless network. It is classified among "R2: Doctoral Universities – High research activity".

Academics

The university features more than 100 undergraduate degrees and 60 graduate degrees, including graduate certificates, master's degrees, and doctorates, through both traditional and online courses. [13] Students attend classes in one of the university's three new colleges and a law school.

  • College of Business, Health, and Human Services
  • College of Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences, and Education
  • Donaghey College of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics
  • William H. Bowen School of Law

Student life at UA Little Rock is characterized by student-run organizations and groups supporting social, academic, athletic, and religious interests. Services include intramural sports, fitness programs, diversity and leadership programs, peer tutoring, student government, support programs for non-traditional and first-generation students, a student newspaper, and Greek life.

The campus’s location near downtown Little Rock allows students to enjoy recreational, entertainment, internship, and job opportunities unique in Arkansas.

Campus Living

UA Little Rock offers various on-campus housing options, including traditional residence halls and multi-bedroom apartments. The university has four residence halls on the east side of campus and the University Village Apartment Complex on the south side.

Students can join themed learning communities focused on:

  • Criminal Justice
  • Arts and Culture
  • Majors and Careers
  • Future Business Innovators
  • Nursing Careers
  • STEM

Athletics

UA Little Rock's 14 athletic teams are called the Little Rock Trojans. Most teams compete in the Sun Belt Conference, except football, which the school has not sponsored since 1955. Athletics are headquartered in the Jack Stephens Center.

Sports Offered

  • Baseball
  • Men's and Women's Basketball
  • Men's and Women's Golf
  • Women's Volleyball
  • Women's Soccer
  • Women's Swimming and Diving
  • Men's and Women's Cross Country
  • Men's and Women's Track and Field (Indoor and Outdoor)
  • Men's Wrestling

Women’s swimming and diving competes in the Missouri Valley Conference, and wrestling competes in the Pac-12 Conference, as the Sun Belt does not sponsor those sports. Wrestling, UA Little Rock’s newest sport, began in 2019 and is the only NCAA Division I wrestling program in Arkansas.

Little Rock joined the Ohio Valley Conference for the 2022–23 season.
